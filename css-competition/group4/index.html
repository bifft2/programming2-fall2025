WEEZER

Weezer is an American rock band formed in Los Angeles in 1992, known for blending catchy melodies with a quirky, geeky aesthetic. Led by frontman Rivers Cuomo, the band achieved mainstream success with their debut self-titled album, commonly referred to as "The Blue Album," featuring hits like "Buddy Holly" and "Say It Ain't So." Weezer's music often combines alternative rock, power pop, and emo influences, and the band is known for exploring themes of isolation, longing, and self-reflection, often with an ironic or humorous twist.
