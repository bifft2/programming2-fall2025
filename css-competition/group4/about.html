About

Weezer, formed in 1992 in Los Angeles, is an influential alternative rock band celebrated for its blend of catchy melodies, introspective lyrics, and a unique, geeky charm. With their debut self-titled album, often called "The Blue Album," they quickly established themselves as a defining force in the genre, thanks to hits like "Buddy Holly" and "Say It Ain't So." Known for their evolution in sound while maintaining their distinctive style, Weezer's discography spans from early, raw power pop to polished, genre-bending experiments. Led by frontman Rivers Cuomo, the band continues to captivate audiences with their engaging performances and inventive music, solidifying their place as one of the most enduring and beloved acts in rock history.
